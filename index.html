Master Syllabus Checklist

Physical World, Units & Measurements
Review fundamental forces, SI units, and dimensional analysis. Practice finding dimensions for constants and solving relative error calculations.
Core
Motion (Straight Line & Plane)
Study v-t and p-t graphs. Derive equations of motion. Decompose vectors, practice projectile motion equations (Time of Flight, Max Height, Range), and solve relative velocity problems.
High Weight
Laws of Motion & Friction
Newton's three laws, conservation of momentum, impulse. Master drawing Free-Body Diagrams (FBDs) for pulleys, inclines, and string tension. Solve static/kinetic friction problems.
Critical
Work, Power & Energy
Work-energy theorem, conservative vs non-conservative forces. Practice conservation of mechanical energy problems, potential energy of springs, and collision mechanics (elastic/inelastic).
High Weight
Systems of Particles & Rotational Motion
Calculate center of mass for rigid bodies. Study torque, angular momentum, and conservation of angular momentum. Practice moment of inertia equations for standard shapes.
Advanced
Gravitation
Kepler's laws, universal law of gravitation. Derive acceleration due to gravity (variation with depth/altitude). Calculate escape velocity and orbital velocity of satellites.
Scoring
Properties of Solids & Fluids
Hooke's law, Young's modulus, stress-strain curve. For fluids: Pascal's law, Archimedes principle, Bernoulli's theorem, viscosity, and surface tension fundamentals.
Formula Heavy
Thermodynamics & Kinetic Theory
Zeroth, First (dQ = dU + dW), and Second laws of thermodynamics. Reversible/irreversible processes. Kinetic theory postulates, ideal gas equation, and RMS speed calculations.
High Weight
Oscillations and Waves
Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM) equations, time period of simple pendulums and spring systems. Wave motion types, speed of traveling waves, superposition, and Doppler effect basics.
Conceptual
Basic Concepts & Atomic Structure
Mole concept, empirical/molecular formulas, limiting reagents. Atomic models (Bohr, Quantum), quantum numbers, electronic configuration rules (Aufbau, Pauli, Hund).
Core
Periodicity & Chemical Bonding
Periodic trends (radius, ionization enthalpy, electronegativity). VSEPR theory, hybridization (sp, sp2, sp3), Molecular Orbital Theory (MOT) for diatomic molecules, hydrogen bonding.
High Weight
States of Matter & Thermodynamics
Gas laws (Boyle, Charles, Avogadro), Ideal gas equation, deviations of real gases. Hess's law of constant heat summation, enthalpy calculations, entropy, and Gibbs free energy conditions.
Numerical
Equilibrium & Redox Reactions
Le Chatelier's principle, Kc and Kp calculations, pH scale, buffer solutions, solubility product. Assigning oxidation numbers, balancing redox reactions via half-reaction method.
Critical
Hydrogen, s-Block & p-Block Elements
Preparation and properties of Hydrogen, water, and hydrogen peroxide. Group 1 & 2 trends and anomalous behaviors. Group 13 & 14 key compounds and structural properties.
Memory Heavy
Organic Chemistry Basics & Hydrocarbons
IUPAC nomenclature, isomerism (structural/stereo), inductive and resonance effects. Alkanes, alkenes, alkynes properties, Markownikoff's rule, and aromaticity (Benzene reactions).
High Weight
Sets, Relations & Functions
Subsets, Venn diagrams, union/intersection operations. Domain and range of relations. Types of functions (polynomial, rational, modulus, signum) and their graphs.
Core
Trigonometric Functions
Radian/degree conversions, sign conventions in quadrants. Sum and difference of two angles, multiple and submultiple angle identities. Solving general trigonometric equations.
High Weight
Complex Numbers & Quadratic Equations
Algebra of complex numbers (a+ib), Argand plane, polar representation. Solving quadratic equations in the complex number system.
Scoring
Linear Inequalities, Permutations & Combinations
Graphical solution of linear inequalities in two variables. Fundamental principle of counting, factorials, nPr and nCr formulas, and application in word problems.
Conceptual
Binomial Theorem, Sequences & Series
Expansion of (a+b)^n, finding general and middle terms. Arithmetic Progression (AP) and Geometric Progression (GP) nth term and sum of n terms. AM and GM relationships.
Numerical
Straight Lines, Conic Sections & 3D Geometry
Slope, intercept, and normal forms of lines. Distance from a point to a line. Equations and standard properties of Parabolas, Ellipses, and Hyperbolas. Distance formula in 3D.
High Weight
Limits, Derivatives & Statistics
Evaluating limits using standard formulas (L'Hospital if applicable). First principle derivatives, product/quotient rules. Calculating Mean Deviation, Variance, and Standard Deviation.
Critical
Introduction & Principles of Electricity
Scope of electronics. Voltage, current, resistance definitions. Ohm's Law, Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL) and Voltage Law (KVL). Practice solving series/parallel resistor circuits.
Core
Network Theorems
Superposition theorem, Thevenin's theorem, Norton's theorem, and Maximum Power Transfer theorem. Practice reducing complex circuits to equivalent single sources.
High Weight
Measuring Instruments
Principles and block diagrams of voltmeters, ammeters, and multimeters. Understanding the Cathode Ray Oscilloscope (CRO) block diagram and measuring amplitude/frequency.
Theoretical
Passive Electronic Components
Color coding of resistors. Types of capacitors and inductors. Energy storage equations. Calculating equivalent capacitance/inductance in series and parallel combinations.
Scoring
AC & DC Applications
Peak, RMS, and average values of AC. Analysis of purely resistive, inductive, and capacitive circuits. Principles of transformers. Basic PN junction diode rectification (Half/Full wave).
Critical
